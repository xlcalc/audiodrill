:title:Irregular Verbs
:keylist: beat, bite, break, buy, cost, dig, hang, know, make, ring, run, sell, spend, wear, win, write

:say: Put the verb "dig" in the right tense:
:write:She (dig) a deep pit yesterday.
:accept:She dug a deep pit yesterday.
:error: dig=> Remember. dig. dug. dug.
::
:say: Put the verb "dig" in the right tense:
:write:The tunnel was (dig) last year.
:accept:The tunnel was dug last year.
:error: dig=> Remember. dig. dug. dug.
::
:say: Put the verb "bite" in the right tense:
:write:The dog (bite) him on the hand.
:accept:The dog bit him on the hand.
:error: biting | bite=> Remember. bite. bit. bitten.
::
:say: Put the verb "bite" in the right tense:
:write:How to stop getting (bite) by mosquitoes?
:accept:How to stop getting bitten by mosquitoes?|How to stop getting bitten by mosquitos?
:show:How to stop getting bitten by mosquito(e)s?
:error: bit | bite| biting => Remember. bite. bit. bitten.
::
:say: Put the verb "beat" in the right tense:
:write:He was (beat) with sticks.
:accept:He was beaten with sticks.
:error: beat | beated| bit| beating => Remember. beat. beat. beaten.
::
:say: Put the verb "beat" in the right tense:
:write:I (beat) three eggs and made an omelet.
:accept:I beat three eggs and made an omelet.|I beat three eggs and made an omelette.|I beat 3 eggs and made an omelet.|I beat 3 eggs and made an omelette.
:show:I beat three eggs and made an omelet/omelette.
:error: beated | bit| beating => Remember. beat. beat. beaten.
::
:say: Put the verbs "hang" and "ring" in the right tense:
:write:She (hang) up on me but I (ring) back immediately.
:accept:She hung up on me but I rang back immediately.|She hung up on me but I ran back immediately.
:show:She hung up on me but I rang back immediately.
:error: hang => Remember. hang. hung. hung.
:error: hanged => Remember. hang. hung. hung. We say "hanged". only when we talk about killing someone.
:error: ring| rung=> In this meaning, the verb's forms are. ring. rang. rung.
::
:say: Put the verb "ring" in the right tense:
:write:I've already (ring) twice to cancel the appointment.
:accept:I've already rung twice to cancel the appointment.|I have already rung twice to cancel the appointment.
:error: ring| rang=> In this meaning, the verb's forms are. ring. rang. rung.
::
:say: Put the verb "hang" in the right tense:
:write:I could see the place on the wall where the picture (hang).
:accept:I could see the place on the wall where the picture had hung.
:error: hang => Remember. hang. hung. hung.
:error: hanged => Remember. hang. hung. hung. We say "hanged". only when we talk about killing someone.
:error: picture hung=> The picture hung before but now it's gone. So it is a past tense but not the simple past.
::
:say: Put the verbs "buy" and "cost" in the right tense:

:accept:I bought this necklace yesterday and it cost almost $800.
:error: bind | buy=> Remember. buy. bought. bought.
:error: costed => Remember. cost. cost. cost. We say "costed". only when we estimate the price.
